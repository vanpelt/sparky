The Inverse-Square Law

Why does a star look faint from far away? Why does a campfire feel less warm as you step back? It all comes down to geometry.

Intensity: 25.0% of original

How it works

Electromagnetic radiation spreads outward from a source in all directions, like an expanding sphere. As the sphere grows, the same total energy is spread over a larger area.

Because a sphere's surface area grows with the square of the radius (A = 4πr²), the intensity — energy per unit area — decreases as the inverse square of distance:

I = P4π r²

Double the distance → ¼ the intensity. Triple it → 1/9th. This applies to all EM radiation: light, radio, X-rays, everything.

Real-world examples

  • The Sun is ~400× farther than the Moon, so sunlight reaching us is far more intense only because the Sun is enormously more luminous
  • Mars receives only ~43% of the solar energy Earth gets, because it's ~1.52× farther from the Sun
  • WiFi signal drops to 25% when you double your distance from the router
  • The Voyager 1 signal is so faint at 24 billion km that NASA needs 70-meter dishes to detect it
  • A camera flash effective at 3m is nearly useless at 10m — only 9% intensity remains
